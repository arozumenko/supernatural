# Combat

Combat in Supernatural is stat-driven and deterministic. Agents assess threats before engaging, form persistent opinions of other agents, and can recruit tamed animals as defenders.

![Combat scene](/images/combat-scene.png)

## Damage Formula

Outgoing melee damage is calculated as:

```
damage = baseDamage * (1 + strength / 20) * (1 + combatSkill / 50) * weaponMultiplier * athleticsBonus
```

Where:
- `baseDamage` -- Base unarmed damage (configurable in world config)
- `strength` -- Agent's STR stat
- `combatSkill` -- Agent's Combat skill level
- `weaponMultiplier` -- Weapon bonus (fists = 1.0, club = 1.3, stone axe = 1.5, iron sword = 2.5)
- `athleticsBonus` -- Small bonus from Athletics skill level

## Defense

Incoming damage is reduced by the Defense skill:

```
damageReduction = defenseSkill / (defenseSkill + 100)
```

This means:
- Defense 0: 0% reduction
- Defense 25: 20% reduction
- Defense 50: 33% reduction
- Defense 100: 50% reduction

Armor items provide additional flat damage reduction on top of the skill-based reduction.

## Confidence System

Before engaging in combat, agents assess threats using a **confidence score**:

- Factors: own combat skill, strength, health, weapon quality, opponent's visible stats, nearby allies
- **High confidence** -- Agent will engage willingly
- **Low confidence** -- Agent will flee or avoid the threat
- Confidence thresholds are defined in the agent's genome and can be modified by [LLM genome patches](/docs/llm-gods)

The Warrior archetype has high fight confidence and the "Stand Ground" strategy rule. The Scout archetype has low fight confidence and the "Early Escape" rule.

## Agent vs Agent Combat

### Disposition System

When two agents meet for the first time, their disposition is determined:

- **Friendly** -- Will not attack, may socialize and trade
- **Neutral** -- Cautious, may become friendly or hostile based on interactions
- **Hostile** -- Will attack on sight

Disposition is **persistent until death** -- once an agent marks another as an enemy, it stays that way for that life.

### Kill Rewards

Killing another agent provides significant rewards:

| Reward | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Massive XP | Large Combat and Defense XP bonus |
| Bonus lives | Gain extra lives (configurable in world config) |
| Loot | Fraction of the victim's inventory drops |

The kill XP reward and loot fraction are configurable in the `combat` section of the world config.

## Cannibalism

Under extreme starvation, agents may resort to hunting other agents for food:

- Triggered when both protein and plant hunger are critically low
- The cannibal agent actively hunts other agents as a food source
- **Witnesses** who see the act mark the cannibal as a **permanent enemy**
- Cannibalism is a desperate last-resort behavior driven by the decision engine's survival priorities

## Alliances

![Taming and alliances](/images/taming-alliance.png)

Agents can form alliances for mutual benefit:

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Max allies per agent | 3 |
| Formation | Based on positive social interactions and relationship thresholds |

### Alliance Benefits

1. **Resource sharing** -- Allied agents share food and materials when one is in need
2. **Mutual defense** -- Allies respond to threats against each other
3. **Confidence boost** -- Having nearby allies increases combat confidence

Alliance thresholds and resource sharing rules are configurable in the `alliance` section of the world config.

## Tamed Animal Defense

Tamed animals (up to 5 per agent) actively defend their owner:

- When the owner is attacked, tamed animals engage the attacker
- Larger tamed animals (dogs, horses) are more effective fighters
- Multiple tamed animals can swarm an attacker
- Tamed animals can die in combat -- they are not replaced automatically

The Social archetype with the "Animal Whisperer" and "Strength in Numbers" strategy rules excels at building an animal entourage for defense.

## Combat Tips

- **Weapon quality matters enormously** -- An iron sword does 2.5x base damage vs fists at 1.0x
- **Defense skill has diminishing returns** -- The first 50 points of Defense are more impactful than the next 50
- **Numbers win** -- Alliances and tamed animals provide a decisive advantage
- **The Warrior genome** starts aggressive but may die frequently to apex predators early on; evolution gradually improves threat assessment
- **Flee early** -- The Scout and Survivor archetypes live longer by avoiding fights they cannot win
